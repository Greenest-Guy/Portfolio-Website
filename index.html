about

Hello! I'm Caleb Greene, a high school senior at Moravian Academy, with an unwavering passion for cybersecurity and programming. Ever since I first got my hands on a computer, I've been fascinated by its complexities and inner workings. I wanted to learn how people could communicate with computers, manipulating them to carry out specific tasks. I was intrigued by how computers can be used to exploit their very own creations. This duality between creation and destruction is what drew me to the field of cybersecurity.

My journey began in middle school with independent study and research. I taught myself Python before entering high school, allowing me to test out of my school's Programming I class and complete Programming II in my freshman year. I continued honing and developing my skills in my sophomore year by learning to use and program a Raspberry Pi, as well as learning Java. In my junior year, I took AP Computer Science A, allowing me to put my Java programming skills to use. I also found my passion for Cybersecurity in my junior year, and began an independent study on vulnerabilities, reverse engineering, and Linux. Now, in my senior year, I'm on track to complete every computer science/programming course my school has to offer, all while continuing my independent study of cybersecurity and planning to complete the CompTIA Security+ Certification.

Aside from technology, I also have a strong passion for music and sports. I play the drums, piano, and bass guitar, with the drums being my main instrument. As of now, I actively play in my school's jazz band and wind ensemble. Jazz is by far the genre im most passionate about; outside of school I've played a countless number of fun jam sessions, and some paid gigs, both with many different musicians from across my community. I have also had the honor of being selected to tour with a jazz band playing in 6 countries in Europe. This was an amazing experience for me, both musically and culturally, allowing me to experience entirely different cultures and explore a wide variety of cuisines. Regarding sports, in my Freshman and sophomore years, I played for my school's Varsity basketball team, transitioning to play Varsity Volleyball my Junior and now Senior year. I have made a lot of new friends and connections through sports, and developed valuable teamwork and teambuilding skills throughout my time playing.
